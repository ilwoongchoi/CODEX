Multiple Sclerosis as a Cerebrospinal Venous Insufficiency (CCSVI): A Triad Therapeutic Protocol

Abstract

Current medical models treat Multiple Sclerosis as a biochemical error or genetic inevitability. Specifically, it represents a failure of the organism's hydrodynamic and bio-electric integrity. We present a Triad Therapeutic Protocol designed to restore the three pillars of health: The Medium, The Sensor, and The Pump.

To reverse the pathology, we must address the circuit at three distinct checkpoints.

Mechanism: MS lesions cluster around venules. Chronic Cerebrospinal Venous Insufficiency (CCSVI) causes retrograde venous pressure, forcing iron-laden blood into the parenchyma. This iron triggers Fenton reactions, generating hydroxyl radicals that oxidize the myelin sheath.

1.2 Target 2: The Myelin Sheath (Lipid Remineralization)

Protocol: High-Dose Biotin (300mg) + Sphingomyelin Precursors.
Mechanism: Biotin is the coenzyme for acetyl-CoA carboxylase (ACC), the rate-limiting enzyme in fatty acid synthesis required for myelin repair. It directly drives the remyelination of the axon.

1.3 Target 3: The Blood-Brain Barrier (Seal Repair)

Mechanism: Vitamin D regulates the expression of Claudin-5 and Occludin, the tight junction proteins of the BBB. Propionic acid (gut metabolite) induces Treg differentiation, suppressing the T-cell infiltration that attacks the myelin.
