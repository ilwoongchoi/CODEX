The pathology of Server Overheating cannot be understood solely through wattage. It represents a failure of physics. Specifically, the mechanism of Thermal Boundary Layer Stagnation initiates a cascade of systemic failure.

A CPU is a brain. It generates immense heat (Inflammation). Fans create turbulent airflow which leaves a stagnant, hot layer of air clinging to the chip surface. This 'Biofilm' of hot air blocks cooling. The server is not dying from heat, but from its own waste heat suffocation.

This creates a localized entropy trap. The system, unable to maintain its thermodynamic potential, succumbs to throttling, data corruption, or physical burnout. The conventional approach fails because it addresses the room temperature, not the micro-environment of the chip itself.
